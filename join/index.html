Why join

More than another school club.

Build experience you can point to, meet students who take ideas seriously, and use what you learn beyond the classroom.

01

Build real skills.

Practice research, data analysis, experimental design, public speaking, and teamwork through work that has a clear purpose.

02

Find your people.

Work alongside curious students with different interests, strengths, and levels of experience.

03

Create local impact.

Help make STEM welcoming and tangible for younger learners through free classroom workshops.

Find your direction

Five ways into STEM.

You can explore one area or move between them. The chapter is a place to discover what holds your attention.

01

Outreach

Train as a presenter and help bring hands-on workshops to Vancouver elementary classrooms.

02

Data & AI

Learn how to ask useful questions, work with evidence, and communicate what the data means.

03

Publication

Turn an idea into a clear scientific argument through research, writing, and peer feedback.

04

Physics

Explore open-ended problems through experimentation, theory, teamwork, and debate.

05

Research

Build the habits behind careful inquiry and learn how to develop a project with support.

Your first steps

Starting is simple.

No prior research, workshop, or presentation experience is required. We care more about curiosity and follow-through.

01

Tell us about yourself.

Complete the short form with your grade and the parts of STEM you want to explore.

02

Meet the chapter.

We will follow up with upcoming meeting details and help you find a comfortable place to begin.

03

Learn by doing.

Join a project, train for a workshop, or start building skills alongside the rest of the team.
